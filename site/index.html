The problem

You get raw material, not study material.

3 hours / course / week

Mechanical prep before any learning

Your professor posts a lecture recording next to an image-only slide deck. Transcribing, matching slides, and writing notes eats hours — before the studying even starts.

no text layer

Slides you can't search

Decks are exported as images. You can't find anything by keyword or quote a slide into your notes.

generic

AI tutors that don't know this lecture

A general chatbot answers about the topic in the abstract. It hasn't heard your professor's recording or seen your deck — so it can't tutor on what's actually tested.

wrong shape

Subscriptions punish irregular use

Tutoring services charge $15–40/month for help you need once or twice per topic. You pay for weeks you don't use.

How it works

Four steps, one master PDF.

Message Aitch on Telegram

Send /start. Aitch discloses — for free — what it does, the commands, and what a session costs. No signup, no card.

Start a session & pay per use

Pay per session in USDC.e via x402 on GOAT mainnet. Below the spend cap Aitch acts autonomously; above it, you confirm explicitly. No subscription.

Send this week's material

Upload the lecture audio (.m4a) and slide deck (.pptx). Your own inputs — nothing locked behind an enterprise data source.

Get your study pack

Audio → transcript, slides → OCR'd searchable PDF, both → structured notes, merged into one master PDF. Then Aitch tutors you on that material.

Why per-session

The pain is weekly and irregular. A subscription charges you for the weeks you skip; per-session pricing only charges when you get a study pack. It's the honest shape for how students actually study.

Session price is always disclosed before any charge. Higher-priced tiers require an explicit typed confirmation before funds move.
